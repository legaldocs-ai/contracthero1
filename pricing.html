Boundaries & fairness
What Aurum does — and what we will never do.
Clear lines protect both you and us. We specialise in calm, honest escalation, not tricks or system evasion.
What we do not do
  • ✕ We do not help you create fake documents, invoices, IDs or proofs of delivery.
  • ✕ We do not encourage “ban evasion”, new accounts to dodge permanent decisions, or violation of platform terms.
  • ✕ We do not log in to your account or pretend to be you in calls or chats.
  • ✕ We do not guarantee outcomes. Platforms always keep final control.

Aurum is built for honest operators who want to talk to platforms in a more structured, professional way — not for people who want to cheat the system.

What you can expect from us instead
  • ✓ We help you describe your real activity clearly, with realistic accountability.
  • ✓ We highlight strong evidence you already have but didn’t know how to present.
  • ✓ We reduce emotional language and contradictions that trigger more risk.
  • ✓ We are honest if we think your case is extremely unlikely to be reversed.

This protects your long-term relationship with platforms and lets you sleep at night.

Questions
Frequently asked before choosing a plan.
Do I pay anything before the diagnosis?

No. The risk diagnosis is free. You see how your case looks from the platform’s perspective and get a preview of the appeal letter before deciding if it’s worth paying for a full letter or pack.

What if my account looks impossible to recover?

If our model believes your case is almost certainly unrecoverable, we will say so. In that scenario, we do not recommend buying a letter just to “try something”.

Can you guarantee that my account will be restored?

No one can guarantee this. Platforms always keep the final decision. Our role is to remove amateur mistakes, present your case calmly, and give you the best possible written position.

Do you talk directly to the platform for me?

No. We do not log in or pretend to be you. You remain the one sending the appeal, but you are now sending a letter that reads like it was written for a risk analyst, not in panic.
