Free risk diagnosis: is your case worth an appeal?

Paste your platform notice and describe what happened. We give you a calm, policy-aware view of your situation: risk level, whether appeal is realistic, and what information you should prepare.

We look at:
  • • Platform type (TikTok, PayPal, Stripe, Facebook Ads, Airbnb, Amazon…)
  • • Common risk triggers: content, payments, KYC, chargebacks, landing page, devices
  • • History: first-time event vs. repeated violations

No login, no screen-share, no password. You stay in control: you paste the notice here and later submit your appeal from your own account.

This runs an automatic risk diagnosis first. For complex or very high-risk cases, we may invite you to a paid full appeal pack.
By submitting, you confirm you are sharing your own case and are not asking for fake documents or account rental. We may decline cases that clearly violate platform or legal rules.

What to expect from a risk diagnosis

This is not a magic unban button. It is a calm, realistic look at your situation so you don't burn your final appeal chance with a random wall of text.

What do I receive after I submit?
You receive a short written diagnosis by email: our best view of what the platform likely saw, how serious it is, and whether it's worth paying for a full appeal pack or VIP support. We sometimes say “do not appeal yet” if your case is not ready.
Can you guarantee my account will be reinstated?
No. Anyone who promises guaranteed reinstatement is not being honest. We can't control internal platform systems or reviewers. We focus on giving you the cleanest, most reasonable version of your story and a structure that respects their policies.
Do you log into my account or send appeals for me?
No. You keep full control. We work through written drafts and clear instructions. You submit appeals or documents from your own devices and accounts. We never ask for passwords, 2FA codes or remote access.
What if my English is not good?
That's exactly why this desk exists. You can write your story in simple English (or mix with your native language if needed), and we will organise it into clear, respectful, policy-aware English that reviewers can understand.
